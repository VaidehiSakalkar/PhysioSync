PHYSIOTHERAPY CLINICAL GUIDELINES — MUSCULOSKELETAL REHABILITATION

=============================================================================
SECTION 1: GENERAL PRINCIPLES OF EXERCISE THERAPY
=============================================================================

1.1 DOSAGE AND PROGRESSION
- Begin with sub-maximal loading (40-60% of maximum voluntary contraction) for acute phases.
- Progress loading by no more than 10% per week (the "10% rule") to minimise overuse injury risk.
- Ensure adequate rest between sessions: 24-48 hours for resistance training.
- Pain during exercise should not exceed 3-4/10 on Numeric Rating Scale (NRS).
- DOMS (delayed-onset muscle soreness) of up to 24-48 hours post-exercise is acceptable.

1.2 REPETITIONS AND SETS (GENERAL)
- Strength (hypertrophy): 3-5 sets × 8-12 reps at 60-80% 1RM, 60-90 sec rest.
- Endurance: 2-3 sets × 15-25 reps at 30-50% 1RM, 30-60 sec rest.
- Motor re-learning/early rehab: 3 sets × 10-15 reps with high quality of movement emphasis.
- Plyometric/power: 3-4 sets × 6-8 reps with full recovery (2-3 min).

1.3 WARM-UP AND COOL-DOWN
- 5-10 minutes of low-intensity aerobic warm-up (e.g., cycling, walking) before exercises.
- Dynamic stretching during warm-up; static stretching (30 s holds) in cool-down.
- Joint mobility drills specific to the target area before resistance exercises.

SECTION 2: KNEE REHABILITATION
=============================================================================

2.1 ANTERIOR CRUCIATE LIGAMENT (ACL) RECONSTRUCTION REHAB

Phase 1 (0-2 weeks): Acute Phase
- Goals: Reduce swelling, restore ROM 0-90°, regain quadriceps activation.
- Exercises: Quad sets, straight leg raises (SLR), heel slides, ankle pumps.
- Weight bearing: Partial weight bearing with crutches as tolerated.
- Ice/elevation 20 min every 2 hours.
- Avoid: Full knee extension under load, deep flexion >90°.

Phase 2 (2-6 weeks): Sub-Acute Phase
- Goals: Full ROM, normal gait, quadriceps strength 50% of contralateral limb.
- Exercises: Mini-squats (0-45°), step-ups (10 cm), leg press (low load), hamstring curls.
- Weight bearing: Full weight bearing; discontinue crutches when normal gait achieved.
- Introduce stationary cycling when ROM >110°.

Phase 3 (6-12 weeks): Strengthening Phase
- Goals: Quadriceps strength 70% of contralateral, single-leg balance 30 sec.
- Exercises: Single-leg press, lunges, Romanian deadlift, lateral band walks, proprioception board.
- Introduce treadmill jogging when quad index >70%.

Phase 4 (3-6 months): Functional / Return-to-Sport Phase
- Goals: Limb symmetry index (LSI) >90% for quad and hamstring strength.
- Exercises: Plyometrics (box jumps, broad jumps), agility ladders, sport-specific drills.
- Criteria for return to sport: LSI >90%, single-leg hop test >90%, pain-free.

RED FLAGS — ACL Rehab:
- Sudden increase in joint effusion (swelling) after exercise.
- Painful clicking or locking of the joint.
- Quad strength plateau over 4 weeks despite training.
- Signs of graft failure: instability during weight bearing.

2.2 PATELLOFEMORAL PAIN SYNDROME (PFPS)

Key Principles:
- Load management: Reduce stairs, squatting, and prolonged sitting with knee bent >90°.
- Strengthen VMO (vastus medialis oblique) to improve patellar tracking.
- Hip abductor and external rotator strengthening reduces valgus stress on patella.

Recommended Exercises:
- Terminal knee extensions (TKE) with band: 3×15.
- Clamshells: 3×15 per side.
- Side-lying hip abduction: 3×15.
- Wall squat with ball between knees: 3×10.
- Step-downs (eccentric focus, 10 cm step): 3×15.
- Straight leg raise with 1-2 kg ankle weight: 3×15.

Avoid Until Pain-Free:
- Deep squats (>90° flexion).
- Running on hills or stairs.
- High-impact plyometrics.

Joint Angle Targets:
- Squat depth: limit to 60° in early rehab, progress to 90° by week 6.
- Optimal terminal extension: 0-5° hyperextension acceptable.

SECTION 3: SHOULDER REHABILITATION
=============================================================================

3.1 ROTATOR CUFF TENDINOPATHY

Load Management Protocol (Jobe's Criteria):
- Begin with isometric exercises in pain-free range.
- Progress to isotonic (concentric + eccentric) when isometric pain-free.
- Eccentric loading is evidence-based for tendinopathy management.

Key Exercises:
- Pendulum (Codman's): 2×30 sec, pain relief focus.
- External rotation with band: 3×15, neutral position.
- Side-lying external rotation (90-90 position): 3×12.
- Scapular retraction/depression: 3×15.
- Prone Y, T, W: 3×10 each.
- Serratus anterior activation (wall push-up plus): 3×15.

ROM Targets:
- Flexion: Full 180° by 8-12 weeks.
- External rotation: 60-90° (depends on dominant side).
- Abduction in plane of scapula (scaption): 120-160° typical.

Red Flags — Shoulder:
- Night pain preventing sleep despite analgesia.
- Deltoid atrophy or weakness in multiple rotator cuff muscles (possible massive tear).
- Cervical spine referral pattern: pain to elbow or below (refer for cervical assessment).
- Failure to progress after 6 weeks of conservative treatment (consider imaging).

3.2 POST-OPERATIVE ROTATOR CUFF REPAIR

Phase 1 (0-4 weeks): Protection Phase
- Arm in sling at all times except exercises.
- Passive ROM only: pendulums, passive flexion to 90°, ER to neutral.
- Elbow, wrist, hand active ROM to prevent stiffness.
- Avoid: Active shoulder elevation, internal rotation behind back.

Phase 2 (4-8 weeks): Active-Assisted Phase
- Active-assisted ROM with pulley or cane.
- Target: Flexion 120°, abduction 90°, ER 40°.
- Isometric strengthening in neutral.

Phase 3 (8-12 weeks): Active Strengthening
- Active ROM to full range; begin isotonic with 0.5-1 kg.
- Scapular stabilisation exercises.
- Avoid overhead lifting >1 kg until 12 weeks.

Phase 4 (3-6 months): Functional Phase
- Progressive resistance: Theraband → free weights.
- Return to overhead activities and sport-specific training.
- Return to contact sport: minimum 6 months, 90% strength symmetry.

SECTION 4: LUMBAR SPINE (LOWER BACK) REHABILITATION
=============================================================================

4.1 NON-SPECIFIC LOWER BACK PAIN

First-Line Treatment:
- Remain active; avoid prolonged bed rest (Grade A evidence).
- Reassurance and education about benign nature of non-specific LBP.
- Supervised exercise program superior to general advice (NICE 2016).

Exercise Hierarchy:
1. Motor control / core stability (Weeks 1-2):
   - Abdominal drawing-in manoeuvre (ADIM): 10×10 sec holds.
   - Dead bug: 3×10 alternating arms/legs.
   - Bird dog: 3×10 per side.

2. Strength progression (Weeks 3-6):
   - Glute bridge: 3×15.
   - Single-leg bridge: 3×12.
   - Plank: progress from 20 sec to 60 sec over 4 weeks.
   - Side plank: 3×20 sec per side.

3. Functional / loaded (Weeks 6-12):
   - Romanian deadlift (RDL): 3×10.
   - Hip hinge with resistance band.
   - Step-ups with load.
   - Farmer's carry.

Joint Angle Targets — Lumbar Spine:
- Flexion ROM: 60-80° (fingertips to mid-shin typical).
- Extension ROM: 20-30°.
- Lateral flexion: 15-25° per side.

Red Flags — Lumbar Spine (refer for urgent medical review):
- Cauda equina syndrome: bilateral leg weakness, saddle anaesthesia, bowel/bladder dysfunction.
- Unexplained weight loss + back pain (possible malignancy).
- Fever + back pain (possible infection/discitis).
- History of cancer with new onset back pain.
- Vertebral fracture risk: osteoporosis, steroid use, significant trauma.
- Night pain not relieved by position change.

4.2 LUMBAR DISC HERNIATION (RADICULOPATHY)

Directional Preference Protocol (McKenzie Method):
- Assess centralisation response: exercises that centralise (move pain proximally) are preferred.
- Most L4/L5 herniations respond to extension bias (prone press-ups).
- Flexion bias exercises for lateral recess stenosis.

Extension Bias Protocol:
- Prone lying: 5×1 min.
- Prone press-ups (cobra): 3×10.
- Standing lumbar extension: 3×10.
- Progress to McKenzie extension in stride standing if response positive.

Avoid:
- Sustained flexion postures (prolonged sitting >30 min without break).
- Loaded spinal flexion (toe touches with weight, forward bending while loading).
- High-impact activities until pain-free.

SECTION 5: HIP REHABILITATION
=============================================================================

5.1 HIP OSTEOARTHRITIS

Conservative Management:
- Aerobic exercise (water aerobics, cycling): 150 min/week moderate intensity.
- Strengthening: Hip abductors, extensors, external rotators.
- Weight reduction if BMI >27 (reduces joint load by 3× per kg lost).

Exercise Protocol:
- Clamshells: 3×15 per side.
- Hip abduction in side-lying: 3×15.
- Mini-squats: 3×15 (pain-free range).
- Step-ups: 3×12.
- Hip extension in prone: 3×15.
- Stationary cycling: 20-30 min at low resistance.

ROM Targets:
- Flexion: 115-125° (loss >30° = severe OA).
- Extension: 10-20°.
- Internal rotation: 20-35° (early loss = hallmark of hip OA).
- External rotation: 40-60°.

Red Flags — Hip:
- Groin pain in <50 year old with limited IR (consider AVN or labral tear).
- Inability to weight bear after minor trauma (possible fracture).
- Night pain, fever, raised CRP (possible septic arthritis — orthopaedic emergency).

5.2 TOTAL HIP ARTHROPLASTY (THA) RECOVERY

Posterior Approach Precautions (6-12 weeks):
- No hip flexion >90°.
- No hip adduction past midline.
- No internal rotation.
- Raised toilet seat, avoid low chairs.

Anterior Approach (fewer restrictions):
- Avoid extremes of external rotation in early phase.
- Weight bearing as tolerated from day 1.

Exercise Progression (both approaches):
- Phase 1 (0-6 weeks): Ankle pumps, quad sets, SLR, heel slides, abduction in lying.
- Phase 2 (6-12 weeks): Bridging, clamshells, mini-squats, gait training.
- Phase 3 (3-6 months): Progressive resistance, stairs, outdoor walking, cycling.
- Return to low-impact sport: 3-6 months; high-impact (tennis): 12 months+.

SECTION 6: ANKLE AND FOOT REHABILITATION
=============================================================================

6.1 LATERAL ANKLE SPRAIN

POLICE Protocol (first 48-72 hours):
- Protection: Air stirrup or bracing.
- Optimal Loading: Early weight bearing as tolerated.
- Ice: 15-20 min, every 2 hours.
- Compression: Bandage.
- Elevation: Above heart level.

Functional Rehabilitation (Week 1-4):
- Alphabet exercises (ankle circles).
- Theraband dorsiflexion, plantarflexion, inversion, eversion: 3×15.
- Calf raises (bilateral → unilateral): 3×15.
- Single-leg balance: 30 sec → eyes closed → BOSU.
- Proprioception board: 3×30 sec.

Criteria for Return to Sport:
- Full pain-free ROM.
- Hop test >90% of contralateral.
- Figure-8 run test <5% asymmetry.
- Single-leg calf raise >25 reps.

6.2 ACHILLES TENDINOPATHY

Eccentric Loading Protocol (Alfredson Protocol):
- Eccentric calf drops off step: 3×15 twice daily.
- Perform even with mild-moderate pain (NRS 3-5 acceptable during exercise).
- Duration: 12 weeks minimum.
- Progress from bilateral → unilateral → add load (backpack/weighted vest).

Isometric Loading (heavy-slow resistance alternative):
- Seated calf raise with heavy load, 5×45 sec holds, 5× per week.
- Evidence: Similar outcomes to Alfredson with less pain during exercise.

Red Flags — Achilles:
- Sudden onset of calf/heel pain with audible 'pop' (possible Achilles rupture — refer urgently).
- Complete loss of plantarflexion power.
- Thompson test positive (no plantarflexion on calf squeeze).

SECTION 7: OUTCOME MEASURES AND ASSESSMENT TOOLS
=============================================================================

7.1 VALIDATED OUTCOME MEASURES

Pain:
- Numeric Rating Scale (NRS) 0-10: simple, widely used.
- VAS (Visual Analogue Scale): more sensitive for research.

Function:
- KOOS (Knee injury and Osteoarthritis Outcome Score): knee.
- DASH (Disabilities of the Arm, Shoulder and Hand): upper limb.
- QDASH (shortened version of DASH): 11 items.
- ODI (Oswestry Disability Index): lumbar spine.
- HOOS (Hip disability and Osteoarthritis Outcome Score): hip.
- FAAM (Foot and Ankle Ability Measure): foot/ankle.

Global Assessment:
- PSFS (Patient-Specific Functional Scale): 3-5 patient-chosen activities, rated 0-10.
- GPE (Global Perceived Effect): -5 to +5 Likert scale.

Performance Tests:
- Y-Balance Test (lower quarter): dynamic balance and injury risk.
- Single-Leg Hop Test: distance, triple hop, crossover hop.
- Timed-Up-and-Go (TUG): mobility in elderly.
- 30-second Sit-to-Stand: lower limb strength.

SECTION 8: PATIENT EDUCATION GUIDELINES
=============================================================================

8.1 PAIN EDUCATION (PAIN NEUROSCIENCE EDUCATION — PNE)
- Pain is an output of the brain, not always proportional to tissue damage.
- Chronic pain involves central sensitisation; education reduces fear-avoidance.
- Explain the difference between 'hurt' (activity-related pain) and 'harm' (tissue damage).
- Use metaphors: smoke alarm analogy — alarm can be oversensitive, not indicating fire.

8.2 POSTURE AND ERGONOMICS
- Sitting: Hips and knees at 90°, feet flat on floor, lumbar support, monitor at eye level.
- Lifting: Hip hinge technique, keep load close to body, engage core before lifting.
- Sleeping: Side-lying with pillow between knees for lumbar protection; avoid prone sleeping.
- Standing: Weight evenly distributed, slight knee flexion, avoid locked knees.

8.3 ACTIVITY PACING
- Boom-bust cycle: Avoid doing too much on good days, leading to flare-up.
- Use a symptom diary to identify activity tolerance windows.
- Quota-based pacing: Set time or repetition quotas regardless of pain level.
- Gradually increase activity quotas each week (5-10% increments).

8.4 SELF-MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES
- Heat for chronic/sub-acute pain and muscle tension (15-20 min).
- Ice for acute pain and post-exercise inflammation (15-20 min, protect skin).
- Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS): 80-100 Hz for pain modulation.
- Mindfulness and relaxation: reduces catastrophising, improves pain coping.
- Sleep hygiene: crucial for tissue healing; target 7-9 hours per night.

SECTION 9: CONTRAINDICATIONS AND PRECAUTIONS
=============================================================================

9.1 ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS TO EXERCISE
- Unstable angina or recent MI (<48 hours).
- Severe aortic stenosis.
- Uncontrolled arrhythmia causing haemodynamic compromise.
- Acute pulmonary embolism.
- Active deep vein thrombosis (DVT).
- Acute infection with systemic features (fever >38.5°C).
- Suspected spinal cord compression.
- Cauda equina syndrome (emergency — refer immediately).

9.2 PRECAUTIONS
- Osteoporosis: Avoid spinal flexion loading; prefer extension and lateral exercises.
- Diabetes: Monitor blood glucose before and after exercise; avoid exercise if >15 mmol/L.
- Hypertension: Avoid Valsalva manoeuvre; monitor BP; isometric exercise caution.
- Pregnancy: Avoid supine position after 20 weeks; avoid high-impact.
- Post-surgical: Adhere strictly to surgeon's weight-bearing and ROM restrictions.
- Corticosteroid injections: Avoid loading the injected structure for 2 weeks.
